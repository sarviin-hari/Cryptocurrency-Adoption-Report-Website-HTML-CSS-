CRYPTOCURRENCY ADOPTION


INTRODUCTION


What is Cryptocurrency?

A cryptocurrency is a digital currency that is represented by an encrypted data string. A peer-to-peer network known as a blockchain monitors and organises it, as well as serving as a secure database of transactions such as buying, selling, and transferring. Cryptocurrencies, unlike actual money, are decentralised, meaning they are not issued by governments or other financial organisations. (Trend Micro, n.d.)


What is this research about?

The following project addresses the relationship between cryptocurrency and the GDP of a country. To conduct a well examined research and a clearer study, 12 countries were chosen from the year 2021 as one of the variables. The 12 countries were categorised as developed, developing and underdeveloped countries. The data for this research was collected from a reliable and trustworthy agency which is International Monetary Fund (GDP of Country) and Triple A (Number of Cryptocurrency Users).


Hypothesis

Country with higher cryptocurrency adoption has increased its GDP value because cryptocurrency provides many incentives for entrepreneurs across the globe

Prediction Country with higher cryptocurrency adoption increases its GDP value
Variable 1 Country
Variable 2 Cryptocurrency Adoption
Variable 3 GDP per capita ($)
Reason for Prediction Cryptocurrency provides many incentives for entrepreneurs across the globe

Data Gathering Techniques

The data that is used in this report is secondary data where the data is collected from an external source such as from organisations or from an individual. The sources used in our analysis is from trustable and reliable organizations so that the data collected is accurate to improve the accuracy of the analysis.

DATA ANALYSIS


The hypothesis presented above shows the relationship between cryptocurrency adoption and the GDP per capita of countries. Cryptocurrency adoption is used to indicate the number of active cryptocurrency users in the world. Higher cryptocurrency adoption in a country, increases the GDP value of a country as cryptocurrency acts as a digital asset to access new demographics, technological efficiencies and international markets. This can increase the involvement of new entrepreneurs in every country which in turn increases the GDP of a country.

The hypothesis shows that countries with low GDP per capita will have lesser cryptocurrency users compared to countries that have a higher GDP per capita. Thus, countries with lower GDP per capita must embrace cryptocurrency as a new digital currency to reap the potential benefits from it. This is important to assist the growth of underdeveloped and developing countries so that the people in these countries can increase their standard of living and increase the economy of their country.

Cryptocurrency adoption for 12 countries were collected from a trustable online source. The percentage of cryptocurrency adoption by country was calculated [Percentage of Cryptocurrency Adoption = (Cryptocurrency adoption/Population of country) *100] for each of the countries. The percentage of cryptocurrency adoption was used to study the relationship with GDP per capita of 12 different countries (developed, developing & underdeveloped). To make sure the data presented is accurate and relatable, all the data was collected from the year 2021.

CONCLUSION


Based on the thorough analysis done in accordance with the visualized data, it can be concluded that the evidence displayed clearly refutes the aforementioned hypothesis that ‘countries with a higher rate of adoption towards the usage of cryptocurrency has recorded positive growth in their GDP per capita’. A number of reasons that further strengthens the argument against this hypothesis is, cryptocurrency hands over citizens control over their capitals by democratizing finance. According to Prasad, 2021 (as cited in Nova, 2021) cryptocurrency also has the potential of destabilizing a country’s financial state if it were to create a massive, uncontrolled financial system with no safeguards for investors. Therefore, it is evident that the emergence of cryptocurrency as a new digital currency technology to the world still lacks trust among stakeholders and people in the current world. Thus, it may have a negative impact on the global economy and GDP of a country.

One of the ways the outcome of this analysis can be improvised is by increasing the number of countries included as samples in this research. The more samples analysed based on a hypothesis constructed, the higher the tendency of the analysis to be accurate because theoretically enough data would have be gathered at the end of the acquisition process to be able to sense a more detailed pattern in the analysis. Besides, a deeper research on cryptocurrency adoption with gross national income of a country can be done to understand how cryptocurrency actually impacts the economy and wages in a country before coming up with a relevant hypothesis. As a concluding statement, based on our analysis, although the usage of cryptocurrency among countries does not benefit the economy of a country, however, theoretically speaking, if more users get their hands on cryptocurrencies, it can be further proven that the advancement of digital currencies can play a vital role in determining the economic state of a country.
